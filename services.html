Cosmetic Eye Surgery

Cosmetic eye surgery, often referred to as oculoplastic surgery, encompasses a variety of surgical procedures designed to improve the appearance of the eyes and surrounding structures, aiming to correct imperfections, rejuvenate the eye area, and in some cases, improve vision.

Vitrectomy

A vitrectomy is a type of eye surgery designed to treat problems of the eye's vitreous and retina. The vitreous is a clear, gel-like substance that fills the middle portion of the eye, while the retina is the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye that is essential for vision.

Cornea Transplantation

Cornea transplantation, also known as keratoplasty, is a surgical procedure to replace part of your cornea with corneal tissue from a donor. It is performed to restore vision, reduce pain, and improve the appearance of a damaged or diseased cornea.

Know more

Neuro-Ophthalmology

Neuro-Ophthalmology is a subspecialty that addresses visual problems related to the nervous system, including visual disturbances caused by brain injuries, stroke, or brain tumors. It bridges the gap between neurology and ophthalmology, focusing on diseases that affect the optic nerve and visual pathways.

Know more

Laser Cataract Surgery

Laser cataract surgery is a modern approach to cataract removal that uses femtosecond laser technology to assist in the surgery, providing a highly precise, safe, and effective alternative to traditional cataract surgery. This method enhances the accuracy of lens placement, potentially improving vision outcomes.

Know more

Oculoplasty

Oculoplasty, or oculoplastic surgery, encompasses a variety of surgical procedures that deal with the orbit (eye socket), eyelids, tear ducts, and the face. It includes both reconstructive and cosmetic surgeries aimed at improving function, comfort, and appearance, addressing conditions from droopy eyelids to orbital tumors.

Know more

Pneumatic Retinopexy (PR)

Pneumatic Retinopexy is a minimally invasive procedure for repairing certain types of retinal detachments. It involves injecting a gas bubble into the eye, which pushes the detached retina back into its proper position, followed by cryopexy or laser photocoagulation to seal the retina in place.

Know more

Photorefractive Keratectomy (PRK)

PRK is a type of refractive eye surgery designed to correct vision issues like myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism. The procedure involves reshaping the cornea using an excimer laser, allowing light entering the eye to be properly focused onto the retina for clearer vision.

Know more

Pinhole Pupiloplasty

Pinhole Pupiloplasty is a surgical procedure aimed at improving visual acuity by creating a pinhole effect through the iris. This can be particularly beneficial for patients with irregular corneas or those who cannot undergo traditional refractive surgery.

Know more

Cryopexy

Cryopexy is a cold therapy procedure used to treat retinal tears and detachments. By applying extreme cold, it creates a scar that helps seal the retina to the underlying tissue, preventing further detachment.

Know more

Retinal Laser Photocoagulation

Retinal Laser Photocoagulation is a treatment for retinal disorders such as diabetic retinopathy and retinal tears. The procedure involves using a laser to create small burns around the area of retinal damage, sealing off tears and reducing leakage from abnormal blood vessels.

Know more

Black Fungus Treatment & Diagnosis

Black Fungus, or Mucormycosis, is a rare but serious fungal infection. Treatment involves antifungal medications, and in severe cases, surgical debridement. Early diagnosis and prompt treatment are crucial for managing this condition effectively.

Know more

Scleral Buckle

The Scleral Buckle procedure is a conventional surgery for repairing retinal detachments. It involves placing a silicone band around the eye, indenting it inward to reduce the traction on the retina, allowing it to reattach to the back of the eye.

Know more

Lasik Surgery

Lasik Surgery is a popular refractive procedure that corrects vision issues like myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism. By reshaping the cornea with an excimer laser, LASIK improves how the eye focuses light rays onto the retina, enhancing visual clarity.

Know more

Glued IOL

Glued IOL is an innovative technique for fixing a dislocated intraocular lens or implanting an IOL in cases where traditional in-the-bag placement is not possible. This method securely attaches the IOL to the sclera, ensuring stability and proper positioning within the eye.
