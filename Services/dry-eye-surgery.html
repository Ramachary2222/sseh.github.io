Dry Eyes

What are Dry Eyes?

Dry eyes occur when your eyes do not produce enough tears or when the tears are not of the correct consistency to keep your eyes adequately lubricated. This condition can lead to discomfort, visual disturbances, and if severe, damage to the surface of the eyes.

Common Causes of Dry Eyes

Several factors contribute to dry eyes, including aging, hormonal changes, environmental conditions like wind and dry air, prolonged use of digital devices, certain medications, and underlying health conditions. Additionally, wearing contact lenses for extended periods and having had refractive eye surgery can increase the risk.

Why Treatment is Necessary

Without adequate treatment, dry eyes can lead to more severe symptoms, including pain, ulcers, or scars on the cornea, and even some loss of vision. Treatment is necessary to restore moisture to the eyes, relieve symptoms, prevent possible complications, and improve quality of life.

Management and Treatment

Management: Mild cases of dry eyes may be managed with over-the-counter artificial tear drops, increasing humidity at home or work, and taking regular breaks during tasks that require prolonged eye focus.

Treatment: For more severe cases, treatments may include prescription eye drops to reduce inflammation, procedures to conserve or produce more tears, and special contact lenses. Lifestyle changes and home remedies also play a crucial role in managing symptoms effectively.
