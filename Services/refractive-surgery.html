Refractive Surgery

What is Refractive Surgery?

Refractive surgery is a group of surgical procedures designed to improve vision and reduce or eliminate the need for glasses or contact lenses. These procedures alter the shape of the cornea or clear front part of the eye, correcting refractive errors such as nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), astigmatism, and presbyopia, to enhance visual acuity.

Common Reasons for Refractive Surgery

Individuals opt for refractive surgery for various reasons, including the desire to decrease dependence on prescription eyeglasses or contact lenses, lifestyle considerations, and occupational requirements. Common refractive errors treated include myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism, and presbyopia.

Why Need Refractive Surgery

Refractive surgery is needed when individuals wish to correct their vision to reduce or eliminate the dependency on eyeglasses or contact lenses. It offers a long-term solution for correcting vision problems, potentially leading to enhanced life quality, greater convenience, and improved visual performance in daily activities.

Before and After Surgery

Before Surgery: A comprehensive eye examination is necessary to determine suitability for refractive surgery. This includes assessing the health of the eye, measuring the refractive error, and discussing the best surgical options based on the individual's eyes and lifestyle needs.

After Surgery: Recovery times vary depending on the type of refractive surgery performed. Most patients experience a rapid improvement in vision, although it may take several weeks to stabilize fully. Postoperative care involves follow-up visits to monitor the healing process and ensure optimal visual outcomes.
