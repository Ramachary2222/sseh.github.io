Laser Cataract Surgery

What is Laser Cataract Surgery?

Laser cataract surgery is an advanced form of cataract removal that utilizes a femtosecond laser to perform steps traditionally done with hand-held instruments. This precision laser technology allows for more accurate incisions, potentially leading to improved surgical outcomes and a higher degree of visual acuity.

Benefits Over Traditional Methods

Compared to traditional cataract surgery, laser-assisted procedures offer several advantages, including enhanced precision in making incisions, reduced energy used inside the eye (which may lead to quicker recovery), and customized treatment tailored to the patient's eye anatomy. These benefits contribute to potentially better vision post-surgery and a more comfortable recovery process.

The Procedure

The laser cataract surgery process begins with detailed imaging of the eye to map its surface and gather crucial information about the lens. The femtosecond laser then creates precise incisions in the eye, softens the cataract, and breaks it up for removal. After the cataract is removed, an intraocular lens (IOL) is inserted into the eye to replace the natural lens, restoring clear vision.

Before and After Surgery

Before Surgery: Patients undergo a comprehensive eye exam, and measurements are taken to select the appropriate intraocular lens. Instructions may be given regarding eating, drinking, and medication adjustments before the procedure.

After Surgery: Recovery from laser cataract surgery is generally quick, with many patients noticing an improvement in vision within a few days. It's important to follow post-operative care instructions, including using prescribed eye drops to prevent infection and inflammation and attending follow-up appointments to monitor healing.
