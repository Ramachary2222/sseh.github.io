Pneumatic Retinopexy (PR)

What is Pneumatic Retinopexy?

Pneumatic Retinopexy (PR) is a minimally invasive outpatient procedure used to treat certain types of retinal detachments. It involves injecting a small bubble of gas into the vitreous cavity of the eye. The gas bubble rises and presses against the area of retinal detachment, helping to reattach the retina to the underlying tissue. This procedure is often followed by laser photocoagulation or cryopexy to seal the retinal tear and secure the retina in place.

Conditions Treated with Pneumatic Retinopexy

PR is specifically used to treat retinal detachments where the tear is located in the upper half of the retina. It is most effective for small or moderate-sized tears and is considered a suitable option for patients where the detachment is not extensive and can be adequately addressed with the gas bubble's positioning.

The Significance of Pneumatic Retinopexy

The procedure offers a less invasive alternative to traditional retinal detachment surgery, often resulting in a quicker recovery time and less discomfort for the patient. Pneumatic Retinopexy can be highly effective in properly selected cases, restoring vision and minimizing the risk of future retinal detachments.

Before and After the Procedure

Before the Procedure: Patients undergo a detailed eye examination to assess the retinal detachment and determine suitability for PR. Instructions may include positioning techniques to help the gas bubble effectively seal the tear.

After the Procedure: Post-procedure care involves maintaining specific head positioning to keep the gas bubble in place, allowing the retina to reattach properly. Activities that could affect the gas bubble's position, such as flying or high-altitude travel, are restricted until the gas bubble dissolves. Follow-up appointments are crucial to monitor healing and ensure the retina remains attached.
